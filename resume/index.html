Women in Scholarship, Engineering, Science, and Technology (WISEST) Summer Research Student
July 2015 - August 2015
As a Summer Research Student, I worked in a Biomechanical Engineering Lab modelling and 3D printing a model of the lower lumbar spine. I was largely responsible for creating the model of the intervertebral disc and printing the distinct parts of the display using MakerBot 3D printers. I also printed and tested small samples of the three materials available for the MakerBot printers. These tests were done according to the standards required for such tests and the results were summarized in a paper that was then presented to my supervisors.

Courses Taken

The following are some of the courses I've taken in university, ordered by date taken from earliest to most recent.

Computing Courses


CMPUT 250
Winter 2018
Computers and Games. This project-based course familiarizes students with the process of creating a game from scratch. The students form teams comprised of three programmers, one artist, one musician, and one writer. I took on the role of programmer and producer for my team, Well-Done Studios, as we used RPGMaker MV to create our game: Debbie's Dog Dilemma. This game is projected for Gold Release on April 12th and the source code is currently posted in the corresponding GitHub Organization repository.

CMPUT 301
Winter 2018
Introduction to Software Engineering. Students form teams of 6 in this project-based course to develop an Android application. I was in charge of notifications and maps and geolocation for this project as well as a large portion of the JavaDocs. While this project is projected for its final release on April 9th, its source code can be found on the Funky Tasks GitHub Organization.

CMPUT 350
Fall 2018
Advanced Games Programming
This course was primarily focused on C++ and basic game AI, such as path finding and attack patterns. CMPUT 350 culminates in a group project wherein teams of 3-4 students make StarCraft II bot. Our bot, ironically named Segfault, was a Terran bot that focused on mid-game strategy. We built a wall to prevent early-game rushes of our base, implemented a priority queue for building upgrades, and used a combination of focus firing and kiting for our attack methods. I was directly responsible for focus firing, kiting, and activating stim-packs.
